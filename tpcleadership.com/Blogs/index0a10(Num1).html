Cracking the code to Resilience

January 28, 2024
2 Min Read
C

The word ‘resilience’ originated from a Latin word resilire, meaning to rebound. It is crucial to build resilience in order to be equivalent to a ‘bouncy ball’ equipped with energy and skills required to emerge stronger from adversity. Moreover, resilience is the ability of an entity to prepare for hardships, rebound from failures and crisis, and to adjust and flourish from struggle. We feel inspired and motivated by numerous resilient people around us. We tend to role model these people because we resonate with their resilience. Hence, people from all walks of life can use the codes developed below to build resilience and harness their internal strengths.

To further build on the relevance of resilience in life, let’s explore the example of floods. The river Nile is the longest river in the world that flows across Egypt. In addition, the river allowed ancient Egyptians to flourish by farming. Throughout the summer season, when all rivers were drying up due to the intense heat, the river Nile covered the whole of Egypt in a flood. Floods disrupt lives by creating enormous loss and destruction. However, floods are vital to restore the fertility of the land for farming. Furthermore, floodplains have several advantages like recharging the groundwater and creating desirable habitats for local wildlife.

Similarly, we encounter unavoidable floods in our lives. So, it is crucial to be prepared and build the skill of resilience before the occurrence of these floods. Also, the way we perceive and respond to the adversity results in whether floods lead to personal growth or negative thought traps. Lastly, there are some hardships that have several positive effects on our lives that we tend to overlook due to our biases.

Firstly in order to be resilient, we need to accept that pain, fear and suffering are inevitable. Our lives are often disrupted by phenomenon like globalization, climate change, economic shocks, civil disturbance and biological weapons. However, resilient humans are capable of extracting wisdom from pain, courage from fear and strength from misery.

Let’s look at the codes which can help us become more resilient:

Code#1- It is essential to change your trajectory in a positive direction when faced by difficult situations/circumstances that are beyond your control.

Once the adversity occurs, the most important thing is acceptance. Next, it is essential to think flexibly and try complex-problem solving methods. We can either give up and sulk during struggle or use strength to recover stronger from a crisis situation. Thus, it can be said that resilience is a choice. We would be tempted to follow the easy path of excuses, accusing and self-pitying. The other path is difficult but transformational, as humans tend to face adverse situations and grow wiser and stronger. We can create the life we desire by taking full responsibility of every moment in our lives. Moreover, accountability makes humans pioneers instead of victims. Having a victim mindset can negatively affect progress and eventually diminishes resilience. On the other hand, a pioneer has the ability to create his/her own path to excellence by adjusting to the hardships.

Code#2- Resilience has to be practiced by making tough choices that lead to excellence.

It is only lack of resilience that acts as a hindrance in converting our dreams into reality. The fundamental necessity of resilient people is to be aware of their emotions. We all experience both positive and negative emotions. Similarly, resilient people cannot numb their negative emotions. However, resilient people are vulnerable and courageous as they accept and openly talk about their feelings. With time we must become aware of the emotions we feel during an adversity and effectively manage these emotions. This is because emotions are our deepest assets if managed and used effectively at all times.

Code#3 -It is important to be emotionally intelligent during hardships.

Of course, it is not possible for us to always act wisely because we are humans. We have tendency of emotional breakdowns during crisis. We are unable to control our emotions, actions and behavior. This often leads to impulse reactions that we may regret in the future. On the other hand, resilient humans are able to accept uncertainty and control impulse reactions. It’s impossible to eliminate impulse but resilience enables us to reflect before taking action.

Code#4 -Avoid making impulsive decisions that can have long-term detrimental effects.

Some of our impulse reactions are based on deep-rooted underlying beliefs. Hence, it is essential to first identify and challenge these beliefs to build resilience. These are mostly general beliefs applicable to varied crisis situations. Mostly, we have deep-rooted beliefs related to achievement, acceptance and control. If we are achievement oriented, then our underlying beliefs are related to success, goals and perfectionism. Conversely, some of us yearn for acceptance and appreciation by others. On the other hand, some of us are control oriented who believe in their ability to control events. These deep-rooted beliefs can subconsciously heighten emotional reactions at times of crisis. Hence, these beliefs need to be transformed for resilience.

Code#5- Identify and change core set of deep-rooted beliefs to effectively respond to crisis situations.

We all have different unique ways of recovering from crisis situations. We tend to have personal resilience needs depending on our preferences, upbringing and beliefs. So, after identifying these resilience needs, we need to determine which relationships help us cultivate resilience. We should spend time and seek guidance from a plethora of people who can provide diverse perspectives on our struggles. You need to realize that you are not alone and guidance from coaches and close friends can help you recover faster as it makes us feel connected. Positive relationships aid us to buffer stressful situations, obtain advice and manage adversity to maintain a sense of balance and well-being in our lives.

Code#6- Substantial investment in relationships broadens our mind-map and possible choices to bounce back from adversity.

Our perspective about the severity of the adversity plays a key role in building resilience. It is important to embody the challenge perspective that allows us to look at struggle as an opportunity for growth and self-improvement. It’s possible to utilize zoom out, a visualization technique to recover from the adversity in our lives.

Simply, zoom out until you reach the moon and have a clear vision of the planet earth. Then, focus on your adversity and maybe you will realize that with all the problems and suffering in the world, your problem isn’t a serious predicament. This results in a change in mindset and our approach to problems in our lives.

Code#7 -Understand the real severity of the adversity by placing your problem into a bigger context.

In conclusion, resilience is a wonderful trait to develop as it improves your realistic optimism, complex problem-solving skill, ability to effectively manage emotions and impulse reactions, and ability to make realistic plans in order to recover from an adversity. With the constant changes in our lives during the worldwide pandemic, we must build resilience in order to sustain and grow. Finally, cultivating the resilience spirit can make you unstoppable and help you emerge stronger from any crisis situation.
